01Core Equations

VIS-VIVA

v² = μ(2/r − 1/a)

Connects orbital speed to distance from the attracting body and the transfer ellipse size.

HOHMANN TIME

T = π√(a_t³/μ)

A one-way transfer is half of the transfer ellipse period.

SOI RADIUS

r_SOI = R(m/M)^(2/5)

The patched-conic boundary where the simulation switches the active gravity source.

ESCAPE BURN

Δv = √(v∞² + 2μ/r) − √(μ/r)

The impulse needed to leave a circular parking orbit with a chosen hyperbolic excess speed.

02Glossary

Patched conics

One-body-at-a-time orbital modeling. The craft follows Earth, Sun, target, or Moon gravity depending on which sphere of influence it is inside.

Synodic wait

The time spent waiting for Earth and the target planet to line up for the return Hohmann transfer. This is why Mars round trips are roughly 2.5 years.

Loose flyby closure

Flybys do not get a free wait or hidden correction burn. The craft may cross Earth's orbit ahead of or behind Earth.

Impulsive Δv

Burns are modeled as instantaneous velocity changes. Real engines burn over minutes and incur losses this version does not model.

03Scope and Limitations

The planets are coplanar and circular, the view is 2D, and the trajectory is precomputed. This version has no manual piloting, fuel mass, inclination, low-energy transfers, aerocapture, atmospheric entry, or N-body perturbations.

Flyby missions do not naturally close on Earth. Pure two-body flybys have no degree of freedom to wait for the return launch window, so this simulator reports the angular offset instead of faking a closed loop. Real free-return trajectories, including Apollo-style lunar returns, use carefully tuned non-Hohmann geometry that is out of scope here.

Orbit-return and touch-return missions may show a residual gap at Earth arrival. That dotted marker is an integration drift artifact from the simplified propagation, not a planned maneuver or a closed-loop claim.

Earth arrival speed is shown as an energy/safety marker. It is not included in total Δv because this version stops at Earth-return geometry and does not model atmospheric reentry, aerocapture, or propulsive Earth-orbit capture.

Touch-and-return descent and ascent are approximate Δv taxes, not surface mission simulations. Planet sizes are exaggerated by default so they remain visible at solar-system scale; true scale makes most bodies collapse to tiny dots.
